## 1. Customer Segment: Who This Is For

Communities, cooperatives, and mission-driven organizations that need capable AI infrastructure and want to govern how it's used together — rather than depend on a single vendor's terms, or build ungoverned AI tooling themselves.

### Jobs to Be Done
- Get reliable access to capable open source AI models without hosting and maintaining infrastructure alone.
- Make fair, legitimate collective decisions as the group scales past the size where "everyone just talks it out" still works.
- Avoid dependency on a vendor whose pricing, policies, or model behavior can change unilaterally.

### Pains
- Centralized AI vendors can change price, availability, or model behavior with no member say in the matter.
- Existing deliberation and voting tools weren't built with AI assistance in mind — or bolt on AI in ways nobody can audit.
- "AI-assisted governance" tools that exist today are largely opaque about whether the AI is quietly shaping outcomes — summarizing selectively, amplifying the loudest voices, or otherwise thumbing the scale.
- Open source self-hosting is real, but doesn't by itself solve *who decides* how the shared infrastructure is used and governed.

### Gains Sought
- Transparency: know exactly what the AI is doing and be able to check its work.
- Fairness: no one's voice — majority or minority — gets quietly discounted.
- Cost control and shared ownership, rather than an ongoing vendor relationship with no equity in it.
- AI assistance that scales deliberation (summarizing, mapping, translating) without displacing real member judgment.

## 2. Value Map: What Sensai and the Agora System Actually Offer

### Products and Services
- Cooperative membership with a real vote and a real economic stake (patronage dividends, not just service access).
- The Agora System: four free, constitutionally-bounded governance functions, plus a general-purpose AI for everyday use, plus an agentic bridge connecting the two.
- Fully open source model weights (OSI-approved / FSF-Free licenses only) — no black-box dependency, ever.

### Pain Relievers
- **Constitutional constraints, enforced in code, not just policy** — the Agora functions cannot rank arguments, cannot rank members, and cannot be changed except by a member vote (Article V, enforced at the technical gateway layer).
- **A dual-run audit trail** pairing every AI input against its output, so any member can independently check whether a summary or map faithfully represents what was actually said.
- **One member, one vote, always** — no subscription tier, donation, or contribution ever converts to additional voting power.
- **Open licensing discipline** — every model in the stack is verified against an OSI-approved or FSF-Free license bar, the same scrutiny applied to every vendor evaluated for this project, so "open source" is never a marketing claim left unchecked.

### Gain Creators
- **Free governance-queue compute**, pre-funded by the treasury, so participating in the cooperative's own decisions is never gated by a member's subscription tier or credit balance.
- **Deliberation Summarizer and Argument Mapper** make it possible to stay meaningfully engaged in governance as membership scales, without reading every comment in every thread.
- **Multilingual Bridge** with hard-routing to human translation for legally material text — accessibility without cutting corners on nuance.
- **Patronage dividends** return operating surplus to members based on usage and contribution, not capital invested.

## 3. Positioning Statement

For communities and organizations that want capable AI infrastructure without giving up their voice in how it's run, Sensai is a member-owned cooperative — unlike a commercial AI vendor, Sensai returns surplus to members, guarantees one-member-one-vote regardless of spend, and runs its own governance decisions through a constitutionally-bounded AI layer, the Agora System, whose every claim is independently auditable rather than simply trusted.

## 4. How This Compares

| | **Centralized AI vendor** | **Self-hosted open source, no governance layer** | **Sensai + Agora System** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing/policy control | Set unilaterally by the vendor | Whoever hosts it decides alone | Set by member vote, one member one vote |
| Model transparency | Usually opaque | Open weights, but no governance tooling | Open weights + constitutionally auditable governance functions |
| Cost model | Extractive (profit to outside investors) | Cost falls on whoever hosts | Shared cost, patronage dividends |
| AI-assisted deliberation | Not typically offered, or offered as an opaque add-on | Not addressed at all | Purpose-built, four narrowly-scoped functions, audit trail included |
| Governance participation cost | N/A | N/A | Free, pre-funded governance queue |
